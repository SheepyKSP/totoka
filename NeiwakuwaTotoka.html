Introduction

Omayo! Welcome to Neiwa kuwa Totoka where you can learn the Totokan language! Totokan is a language a part of the Tokic language and is one the the official languages in Hiya spoken as a first language in the Totokan islands located off the coast from the autonomous region of Fríndland. The capital is Motokashi and it is home to the Totokan language office. Now enjoy learning our language!

Greetings

  • Omayo (Hello)
  • Ukogayo (Thank you)
  • Oaio (Good morning)
  • Uaio (Good night)
  • Sayon (Bye)

Word order

The word order is quite simple, it is as follows:

Tense - Subject - Verb - Object

As you may notice this is the same as in English. The only difference is the added tense which you will learn about in the Conjugation section

Conjugation

In Totokan conjugation works by using one of the 3 conjugation words

  • Saiwa (Past)
  • Naiwa (Present)
  • Kaiwa (Future)

An important thing to note is that ALL verbs end in “-wa”, the endings of words can be very important to find the meaning or a deeper meaning to a sentence.
